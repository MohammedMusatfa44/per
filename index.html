Learn Programm

Before entering the world of programming, there are many basics that one must learn, including: Learning to use the computer: You must know how to use the computer correctly to solve problems and to avoid future errors during programming. Understanding the concept of programming: One must understand the basics of programming and how computer code works to carry out tasks. Studying Programming Languages: One must study a specific programming language and understand the language's grammar, syntax, and explanation of codes. Self-learning: One must be able to self-learn and search for information needed to solve the problems he or she faces. Mathematics: Programming includes many mathematical concepts such as algebra and statistics, so studying mathematics in general will help to better understand and analyze problems. Computer literacy: One must understand computer basics such as operating systems, networks, and computer hardware in general. Practice and Training: One should practice programming regularly and practice writing codes and solving problems. Communication and Collaboration: One must be able to communicate and collaborate with the business team to solve problems and improve the program. In general, you must know the use of the computer and its tools, and you must be open to the Internet and social media, and you must search for information yourself on an ongoing basis to learn programming well

Electronic extortion: its threats, forms, and methods

Electronic extortion: its threats, forms, and methods Electronic extortion is a type of cybercrime represented in the use of electronic means of communication to threaten individuals or institutions with the aim of obtaining money or confidential information or achieving other illegitimate goals. Electronic extortion includes a variety of forms and methods, and here we will learn about ten foundations that help us understand this phenomenon: Informational threats: extortionists threaten victims with revealing private or embarrassing information if they do not pay a certain amount of money.Electronic extortion: its threats, forms, and methods Leaking personal photos and videos: The blackmailer can use photos or videos of the victims and threaten them to post them on social media if they do not meet his demands. Identity exploitation: Extortionists sometimes claim to have information about victims and use that information to blackmail them. Threat of economic harm: The extortionist can threaten economic harm to the victim by damaging her reputation or publishing false information about her.Ransomware attacks: extortionists use malware to infiltrate computer systems and encrypt files, after which the victim demands a ransom in exchange for recovering their files. Use of Social Networks: Extortionists can collect information about individuals through social media platforms and use it to blackmail them. Threat of Defamation: The blackmailer can threaten to publish misleading or false information about the victim with the aim of discrediting her. Emotional manipulation: extortionists use emotions and fear to achieve their goals, such as threatening to harm the victim's loved ones. Phishing: A blackmailer can use phishing emails to defraud individuals and obtain sensitive personal or banking information.
In the face of these threats, it is important for individuals and organizations to be aware of the methods of cyber extortion and take the necessary measures to protect themselves by updating their security software and not sharing sensitive personal information with untrusted parties.

Seven sins of programmers

Pride: Believing you are better than others and not accepting criticism or improvement. Vanity can lead to neglecting good design and ignoring the possibility of errors. Envy: Being jealous of success or other skills and focusing energy on trying to outdo others without focusing on developing your own. Anger: reacting negatively to problems or difficulties during programming, which leads to a breakdown of focus and illogical decisions. Sloth: Not working hard or failing to achieve your full potential in programming. Laziness can lead to delays in completing projects and a decline in the quality of work Greed: Excessive desire to gain more profits or fame, which may lead to rushing the implementation of projects or sacrificing quality in order to achieve these desires. Lust: Excessive appropriation of new technologies or excessive attachment to certain tools without caring whether those technologies are really effective for solving problems. Human instincts (gluttony): Your impulsiveness to do unnecessary tasks or accumulate more information without making real progress. This can lead to distraction and loss of time
